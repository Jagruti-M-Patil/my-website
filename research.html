Research

My research focuses on quantitative ultrasound (QUS) methods that extract information about tissue microstructure from backscattered echoes. I develop and validate approaches based on envelope statistics (e.g., Nakagami and Burr-family models) and H-scan imaging to characterize soft tissue and support better differentiation between tissue states.

Research Themes

Theme 1
Envelope Statistics & Scattering Models
  • Modeling echo-envelope distributions to quantify deviations from Rayleigh-like scattering.
  • Parameter estimation and fitting strategies for robust, reproducible QUS biomarkers.
  • Interpreting parameter trends in terms of microstructural heterogeneity and scatterer statistics.

Representative Applications

  • Brain tissue (ex-vivo): differentiating glioblastoma and normal tissue using QUS markers derived from envelope statistics.
  • Oral lesion progression (preclinical): tracking microstructural and scattering changes using H-scan metrics and statistical parameters.
  • Ocular tissue (research/translation): envelope-statistics based characterization for choroidal tumor differentiation (project context).
